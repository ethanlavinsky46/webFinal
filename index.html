What is Interactive Storytelling?

Interactive stories/branching narratives are stories that don't follow a linear progression. They diverge into multiple paths, meaning that the audience has to make choices in order to progress in the story. There will be more than one possible ending that the audience may reach. These types of stories can be found in mediums such as print, film, and digital games.

Why do people like Interactive Storytelling?

Two of the main drawing points to these types of stories are the sense of agency and immersion they provide for their readers. In the case of Choose Your Own Adventure books, the children who read them were able to insert themselves as the protagonist in exciting scenarios like traveling through space or time since their actions had discernable consequences on their perception of the narrative. Thus, these books can foster a sense of agency for the readers while still being easy going in regards to the consequences of their choices, since the possibilities of the stories are pre-determined by the writers and the conflict of these narratives are separate from the real world.
