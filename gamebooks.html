Game Books

Branching narratives truly came into their own through the emergence of game books like the Choose Your Own Adventure Series. In this type of branching narrative, the reader is presented with choices that determine the outcome of the protagonist, and thus the reader assumes the role of the protagonist and decides their fate within the constraints of limited pre-written endings that they are encouraged to explore.


Consider the Consequences (1930)

Doris Webster

One of the earliest known gamebooks. A romantic novel where you can adapt the viewpoint of one of 3 characters.

The Cave of Time (1979)

Edward Packard

The reader is presented with the discovery of a cave that can take you to various points throughout history depending on the page you choose to advance to when inquired.


Dungeon of Dread (1982)

Rose Estes

Part of the Endless Quest Series, in which the reader is directly referred to as "YOU". The protagonist is a knight named Caric who challenges an evil wizard.

Meanwhile (2010)

Jason Shiga

A comic that lets readers pick what panel to advance to through a complex visual system of tabs and “wires” that allow for 3,856 possible stories to experience.
