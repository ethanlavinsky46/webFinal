Interactive Movies

Interactive storytelling continued to evolve when it became utilized in the medium of film. This was done through the use of multiple cuts and curated audience participation. And in recent years, this type of film became more accessible through digital platforms in which the choices prompted to the audience are pre-programmed.

Kinoautomat (1967)

The very first interative movie.The audience had buttons in front of their seats that allowed them to make decisions dictated by the narrator that determined which of the two pre-filmed cuts of the movie would be shown to them

Dragon's Lair (1983)

Widely considered the first interactive animated cartoon. It used laser disc technology and arcade machine controls to determine how the animation would play out.

Black Mirror: Bandersnatch (2018)

An interactive sci-fi/drama film on Netflix in which the viewer can make choices for the protagonist, Stefan Butler, a programmer who is making a video game based on a "Choose Your Own Adventure" book

Batman: Death in the Family (2020)

An interactive animated short film with 9 story paths and 7 alternate endings, determined by the choices the viewer makes in regards to the fate of Jason Todd (aka Batman's sidekick, Robin)
